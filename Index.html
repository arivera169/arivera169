What Am I?

In 2021, I got my Bachelor's degree in Environmental Planning & Design with a minor in managment from the Universty of New Mexico. From there I went to work for Roadrunner Food bank as a Community Outreach Coordinator. I worked for several different factions within Roadrunner Food bank. Within my position, I was able to work with large amounts of data on both the input and the analysis of data. I worked with volunteers and customers alike in large volumes on a weekly basis. In the course of my time there I discovered an underlying love for working with data. Data in my eyes is the fundamental requirements for making important high-level decisions and allows for the ability to carryout those decisions in a time and resource efficient manner. Since my background was in Environmental Planning & Design I thought about the possibility of finding a level of intersectionality between data and planning. Within my internship with the Bureau of Reclamation, I have learned a great number of skills but the highlight of that has been my expanding knowledge of GIS. Within that sphere the skills that I have grown to love is working with mobile ESRI applications to deploy accurate field data collection. I am passionate about food access and have created a website local to New Mexico with the help of my team. Food Map
